BYLAWS

OF THE WESTERN ARIZONA ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT DISTRICT

Adopted September 9, 2004
Revised August 10, 2020

Statement of Principles and Policies:

The underlying concept of the Economic Development District (the DISTRICT) is that cities, towns, and counties, which are most familiar with their particular socio-economic conditions, should exercise the primary responsibility for treating those problems or needs through a countywide or regional planned strategy.

The area of concern for the Economic Development District is defined as the area lying within the geographic boundaries of La Paz, Mohave, and Yuma Counties (Counties).

Programs and projects to address socio-economic problems of local government shall be developed in regular meetings of each County per the policies developed by the District Organization Committee.

The District is based on the principle of equality of membership. Nothing contained in these BYLAWS shall authorize the District Organization Committee to intervene in matters which are mostly within the jurisdiction of any one (1) member without its consent.

The DISTRICT is not, nor is it intended to be, a substitute for local government. It is, however, an organization through which individual governmental units can work on and resolve regional problems and coordinate their efforts

ARTICLE 1 - FUNCTIONS

The general functions of the DISTRICT are as follows:

1.1 To provide a forum for discussion and evaluation of economic problems and opportunities of mutual interest and concern to the governments within the Counties (La Paz, Mohave, and Yuma).

1.2 To ensure, through cooperation and the combined resources, maximum efficiency, and economy in governmental operations, which will provide every citizen with the utmost value for every tax dollar.

1.3 To understand, clarify, identify, and comprehensively plan for the solution of regional problems requiring jurisdictional cooperation.

1.4 To facilitate agreements among the governmental units on specific projects or other interrelated development actions or for the adoption of standard policies concerning issues that are common to its members.

1.5 To attain the highest degree of intergovernmental cooperation possible to prepare for future growth and development of the Counties.

1.6 Other functions - such other countywide or regional functions as the membership shall deem appropriate for the Association and the District.

ARTICLE 5 – DISTRICT ORGANIZATION

5.1 The District Organization Committee governing body is broadly representative of the principal economic interests of the District, which includes the private sector, public officials, community leaders, representatives of workforce development boards, institutions of higher education, minority and labor groups, and private individuals. The District Organization Committee will maintain the capacity to implement the EDA-accepted CEDS, per Title 13 Code of Federal Regulations (CFR) § 304.2 - District Organizations: Formation, organizational requirements, and operations.

The powers and functions of the District Organization Committee and its members, subject to the limitations from now on stated, shall include:

  • 5.1.1 The formulation of policy decisions and the determination of policy matters for the Economic Development District.
  • 5.1.2 The approval and adoption of a budget for each fiscal year and a dues schedule.
  • 5.1.3 The initiation and request for studies to be undertaken either by interagency agreement, contract, or otherwise as they may deem appropriate.
  • 5.1.4 The right of any official representative, at any meeting of the District Organization, to propose a subject for study by the Economic Development District.
  • 5.1.5 The right of any official representative at any meeting of the District Organization Committee to request a review on any action taken by the Management Committee during the interval between District Organization Committee meetings.
  • 5.1.6 The appointment of such standing and special committees deemed necessary to achieve the purposes of the Economic Development District.
  • 5.1.7 No member of the District Organization Committee shall have the authority to bind the Economic Development District, act on its behalf, nor use its name unless officially granted such authority by the Executive Council.Paul: Executive Council needs to be defined in Article 2.

Rights and Liabilities of Members:

  • 5.2 Individual members and their representatives are not liable or responsible for any debt(s) or liabilities incurred by the District . Additionally, District will provide liability protection for the District Organization, its staff, and members.
  • 5.3 The District Organization Committee will make no person or private party to District Organization Committee members or their families, District Organization Committee Management, Committee, Members or their families, or District Organization Committee Staff or their families. ARTICLE 6 - MANAGEMENT COMMITTEE

6.1 A Management Committee shall be established consisting of one official representative from each County's Board of Supervisors.

6.2 The Management Committee shall be responsible for the functions as from now on set forth:

  • 6.2.1 There shall be selected a Chairman and Vice-Chairman from the members of the Management Committee. Said election shall occur at the first meeting after January 1 of each year. In the event a vacancy occurs in the chairmanship, then the Vice-Chairman shall become Chairman for the unexpired term, and a Vice-Chairman shall be elected to complete the remainder of the Vice-Chairman's term.
  • 6.2.2 The Management Committee shall have the authority to recommend to the District Organization, committees, and personnel to study specific problems, programs, or other matters which the Management Committee or District Organization Committee has approved for study.
  • 6.2.3 The Management Committee shall act as the coordinating agency for all other committees.
  • 6.2.4 The Management Committee shall keep the District Organization Committee informed on any matter or problem involving intergovernmental cooperation.
  • 6.2.5 The Management Committee shall perform any other functions assigned by the District Organization Committee.
  • 6.2.6 A vacancy on the Management Committee shall be filled by the respective County whose representative vacated the position on the Management Committee.

ARTICLE 7 - STANDING AND SPECIAL COMMITTEES

7.1 Standing and Special Committees shall be created by the District Organization Committee from time to time as it may deem appropriate.

7.2 The District Organization Committee shall authorize and define the powers and duties of all Standing and Special Committees of the District.

7.3 The District Organization Committee shall designate a Chairman and Vice-Chairman of the Standing and Special Committees. The District Organization Committee shall fill vacancies occurring in these positions.

7.4 The District Organization Committee shall determine membership on Standing and Special Committees. There shall be no minimum or a maximum number of members on any Standing or Special Committee. Nothing in these BYLAWS shall be construed to limit membership on these committees above exclusively to officials serving political subdivisions of the State. The District Organization, in its discretion, may appoint any individual it deems qualified to serve on a Standing or Special Committee. ARTICLE 8 - VOTING

8.1 Voting on the District Organization Committee and the Management Committee shall be conducted in the following manner:

  • 8.1.1 A simple majority of the membership shall constitute a quorum.
  • 8.1.2 In District Organization Committee meetings, each member shall have one (1) vote. In Management Committee meetings, each member shall have one (1) vote.
  • 8.1.3 When a quorum is present at any meeting, a majority of those present shall decide any questions brought before such meeting.
  • 8.1.4 The Chairman of the District Organization Committee and the Chairman of the Management Committee shall have the same voting privileges as regular members of their respective bodies.

8.2 Governing bodies may designate an alternate to represent said member on the Executive Board or the Management Committee; however, such designation must be in writing. The said written designations shall be forwarded to the Chairman and kept on file by the Executive Director.

8.3 An Official Representative or Alternate Representative shall vote only in person. There shall be no proxy voting.

ARTICLE 10 - FINANCES

10.1 Fiscal Year - The fiscal year of the Economic Development District shall commence on July 1 and end the following June 30.

10.2 The District Organization Committee and its Executive Board must comply with all Federal and State financial assistance reporting requirements and the conflicts of interest provisions set forth in accordance with Title 13 Code of Federal Regulations (CFR) § 302.17.

10.3 The Economic Development District shall have the power to receive bequests, donations, grants, and gifts of all kinds of property, in fee simple, and to do all acts necessary to carry out the purposes of such bequests, gifts, grants, and donations, with power to manage, sell, convey, lease or otherwise dispose of the same per the terms of the estate, grant, gift, donation or trust be unconditional.

10.4 The dues assessed for all eligible regular members shall be established on a per capita basis based on the most recent decennial U.S. Census, provided that dues assessed for city and towns shall be based on population within their corporate limits, and the dues assessed for counties shall be based on population in the unincorporated area of the counties.

10.5 The District Organization Committee may establish special assessments for members of the Economic Development District for special studies or projects, sufficient to provide the funds required for the completion of said studies and projects.

10.6 Any member whose annual dues assessments have not been paid within three (3) months after the commencement of any new fiscal year shall not be entitled to vote or participate at such a meeting or any subsequent meeting until the member pays said dues.

10.7 The Executive Director shall cause an annual audit of the financial affairs of the Economic Development District and the Corporation to be made by a certified public accountant at the end of each fiscal year. The audit report shall be made available to all members.

10.8 The Economic Development District 's program allocations will be based on need when the agreement is possible; otherwise, it will be on a proportionate basis based on the decennial population.

ARTICLE 11 – OPERATIONS

11.1 The District Organization Committee shall engage in the full range of economic development activities listed in its EDA-accepted CEDS.

These activities may include:

  • 11.1.1 Coordinating and implementing economic development activities in the District;
  • 11.1.2 Carrying out economic development research, planning, implementation and advisory functions identified in the CEDS; and
  • 11.1.3 Coordinating the development and implementation of the CEDS with other local, State, Federal, and private organizations.

11.2 The District Organization Committee may at its option contract for services to accomplish the activities.

ARTICLE 12 - EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR

12.1 The District Organization Committee shall select the Executive Director of the Economic Development District. The Executive Director shall furnish the necessary staff support to perform its economic development functions and its authorities and responsibilities for carrying out economic development functions of the Economic Development District.

12.2 The Executive Director shall maintain the correspondence, library, meeting minutes, and records of the Economic Development District.

ARTICLE 13 - WITHDRAWAL

13.1 Any member may withdraw from the Economic Development District provided that the intent to withdraw is stated in the form of a resolution adopted by the legislative body of the jurisdiction wishing to withdraw. Receipt of the resolution by the Executive Director in the appropriate form shall be deemed to be notified of withdrawal, which shall be effective on the next June 30.

ARTICLE 14 - AMENDMENTS

14.1 Amendments to these BYLAWS may be proposed by any member of the District Organization Committee and may be amended by a vote in the same manner as any other question, provided the proposed amendment shall have been submitted in writing to the Secretary/Treasurer at least thirty (30) days before the meeting at which action is intended, and the Secretary/Treasurer shall submit the proposed amendment to each member at least fifteen (15) days before said meeting.

ARTICLE 15 - EFFECTIVE DATE

15.1 - These BYLAWS shall become effective immediately upon the passage of the resolution by a majority of the membership in the Economic Development District.
